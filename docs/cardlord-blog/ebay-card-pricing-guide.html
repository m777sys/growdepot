How to Research Sold Prices (The Right Way)

Most sellers open eBay, search their card, and look at the lowest "Buy It Now" price. That's wrong. Here's what to do instead:

Step 1: Use eBay's "Sold" Filter

  1. Search your exact card (set number, condition, grading)
  2. Click "Filters" → "Show only" → "Sold items"
  3. Set date range to "Last 30 days"
  4. Sort by "Sold Date" (newest first)
Pro Tip: Look at the last 10-15 sales, not just the lowest. Cards that sold at auction often go 10-25% lower than Buy It Now sales. Use the median, not the average.

Step 2: Account for Subtle Differences

Two identical cards can have different values based on:

  • Condition specifics: Near Mint vs. Lightly Played can be 30-50% difference
  • Grader: PSA 10 vs. CGC 10 can be 20%+ different for premium cards
  • Sell speed: Cards that sell within 24 hours = underpriced. Cards that relist 3x = overpriced
  • Listing quality: Good photos sell 40% faster. Factor this in.

Step 3: Check PriceCharting as Backup

PriceCharting aggregates eBay sales but has a 24-48 hour delay. Use it for:

  • 3-month price trends (is it rising or falling?)
  • Ungraded cards (more data points than PSA sites)
  • Quick reference when you can't access eBay
Warning: PriceCharting includes "Best Offer accepted" prices — which are often lower than shown. Always verify with actual eBay sold listings for cards over $50.

The Real Fee Formula

Calculate Your Net Profit

Take-Home = Sale Price × 0.82 − Shipping Cost − $0.35

(Assumes 14.95% FVF + ~5% for promoted standard + insertion)

Example: You sell a PSA 10 Charizard for $200

• eBay fees (18%): −$36
• Shipping with tracking: −$15
• Net to you: $149

Most sellers think "I made $200" when they really made $149 (before cost of goods).

Price for Speed vs. Profit

There's no single "right" price. It depends on your goal:

Strategy Price at Sells in Best for
Quick Flip 5-10% below market 1-3 days Fast cash, hot cards
Market Rate Median sold price 1-2 weeks Standard inventory
Maximize 10-20% above market 3-8 weeks Rare / trending cards
Set & Forget Buy It Now only 1-3 months Long-tail inventory
Velocity Check: If a card gets 10+ watchers in 48 hours, you're underpriced. If zero watchers after 7 days, you're 20%+ overpriced.

How Grading Affects Price

PSA 10 vs Ungraded Multipliers

These are average multipliers for modern cards (2020+):

Card Type Ungraded Price PSA 10 Multiplier
Common Holo (Pokémon) $2-5 4-8x ($15-40)
Secret Rare (One Piece) $50-100 2-4x ($150-350)
Alt Art (Pokémon) $20-80 3-6x ($100-400)
Manga Rare (One Piece) $200-500 2-3x ($500-1500)

When to Grade vs. Sell Raw

Grade if:

  • Ungraded value is $30+ and PSA 10 multiplier is 3x+
  • Card looks flawless under 10x magnification
  • It's a rare / limited card that won't get reprinted

Sell raw if:

  • Ungraded value is under $20 (grading costs eat profit)
  • Card has centering issues or surface wear
  • You're flipping fast and need cash now

Grading ROI Calculator

Grade if: (PSA 10 Sale Price × 0.82) − $40 grading cost > Ungraded Sale Price × 0.82

Don't grade cards worth less than $30 ungraded unless they're sure-fire 10s.

When to Drop Your Price

Don't let cards sit stale. Here's the relist schedule:

  • 0 watchers after 7 days: Drop 15-20% (you're way overpriced)
  • 1-2 watchers after 14 days: Drop 10% (slightly overpriced)
  • 3-5 watchers, no sale after 21 days: Drop 5-7% (fair price, weak demand)
  • Send offers to watchers: 10-15% below list price every 5 days
Relist Strategy Example:

Week 1: List at market rate ($100)
Week 2: Drop to $90 if no watchers
Week 3: Drop to $85, send $80 offer to watchers
Week 4: Drop to $80 or delist and wait for market to move

Key: Always update your comps before dropping. If market prices dropped 20%, you're not overpriced — you're just holding a declining card.

Seasonal Price Changes

Card prices are cyclical. Know the patterns:

Time Pokémon Trend One Piece Trend
January-March Stable / slight dip Pre-set release speculation
April-June Graduation gift surge (+15%) New set hype peak
July-August Summer lull (-10%) Post-release correction
September-November Holiday ramp-up (+20%) Holiday + tournament season
December Peak prices, then crash Gift buyers active
Sell Strategy: List high-value cards (PSA 10s, $100+) in October-November for holiday buyers. Buy undervalued inventory in January-February when sellers are cash-strapped after Christmas.
